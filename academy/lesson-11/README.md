# Lesson 11: Developing Your Trading Plan

**Seekapa Trading Academy - Premium Finance Education Module**

## Overview

This lesson teaches traders how to create personalized, comprehensive trading plans that serve as their business constitution. Students learn to define SMART goals, establish mechanical entry/exit rules, test strategies through backtesting and paper trading, and implement continuous improvement processes.

## Learning Outcomes

After completing this lesson, students will be able to:

1. **Create Personalized Trading Plans** - Develop a complete trading blueprint defining their approach
2. **Set Realistic Goals** - Use SMART framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) for trading targets
3. **Document Trading Rules** - Define objective entry/exit criteria with specific parameters
4. **Review & Refine Plans** - Implement monthly review process for continuous improvement

### Content Sections

#### 1. Trading Plan Components & Structure (540 words)
Learn the 6 essential components every trading plan must include:
- Trading goals and SMART framework
- Risk tolerance definition
- Preferred markets selection
- Entry and exit criteria
- Position sizing formula
- Review schedule

#### 2. Defining Entry and Exit Rules (520 words)
Master objective, mechanical trading criteria:
- Specific entry conditions (price action, indicators, volume)
- Multiple exit strategies (profit targets, stop losses)
- Reward/risk ratio optimization
- Scenario-based rule documentation
- Prevention of emotional decision-making

#### 3. Backtesting and Paper Trading (530 words)
Test strategy validity before risking real capital:
- Historical data analysis methodology
- Performance metrics evaluation
- Real-time market practice
- Strength and weakness identification
- Multiple market condition testing

#### 4. Plan Review & Continuous Improvement (530 words)
Evolve and optimize your trading approach:
- Monthly performance review process
- Metrics assessment and tracking
- Position sizing adjustments
- Entry/exit criteria refinement
- Data-driven improvement methodology
